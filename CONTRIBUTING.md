# Contributing

Two channels, by purpose. **Issues** carry concrete proposals: a specific statement to change, a failure mode to add, a profile to contribute. **Discussions** carry open-ended debate during the review period. Both work best when they cite statement identifiers (M1, O1, S1, P1, PS1, C1, MS1, AP1), which is what the identifiers exist for, and the framework-feedback issue template is pre-shaped for exactly that.

## Contributing an archetype profile

Profiles are the framework's primary contribution surface. The bar a profile must meet is AP1 to AP5 in `profiles/README.md`, and `profiles/TEMPLATE.md` is the file to copy.

1. Copy the template to `profiles/<archetype-slug>.md` (example: `regulated-enterprise.md`).
2. Fill every section. AP3 means shoulds may be adjusted with recorded rationale, and musts are never weakened.
3. Submit as a pull request, or open a profile-proposal issue first if you want direction before writing.

Review works as follows: the maintainer acknowledges within a week, reviews against AP1 to AP5 within a month, and records the disposition on the pull request or issue. Accepted profiles are credited to their authors.

## Changing the framework text

Editorial fixes (typos, formatting, broken links) come straight in as pull requests. Changes to normative text follow the process in `GOVERNANCE.md`.
